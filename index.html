About

"A Master Bowyer has broken more bows than a new bowyer has ever attempted."

Why Traditional?

Nothing feels quite the same as a traditional bow in your hand. No sites to tell you where to shoot. No pulleys to adjust. Just you and the bow. I have found that every bow I make has a character. When the Archer and the bow first come together, they start to become one with each other. The bow draws from the instinct of the Archer and the Archer learns the feel of the bow. There is nothing quite like shooting a traditional bow. Only way to know is to try it for yourself.

History

As a kid, I have always had a fascination with bows. My first bow was a branch of a tree with some elastic cord attached at both ends. Since then, my curiosity has only grown. I really started digging into bow making when I was 16 when I made my first successful bow.
